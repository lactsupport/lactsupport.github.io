Who to call for breastfeeding support
Scope of practice guide for parents · Different providers have different training and authority — use this to find the right help faster.
Important: Providers at each level can refer you up. Your CLC or IBCLC may recommend you see a physician if they identify a concern that requires diagnosis or prescription treatment. When in doubt, contact whoever you can reach first — escalation is a feature, not a failure.

Mastitis
Breast redness, warmth, fever, flu-like symptoms
Can help
  • Encourage rest & hydration
  • Emotional support
  • Urge prompt care-seeking
Urgent: recommend provider contact
Supportive role
  • Effective milk removal guidance
  • Positioning to drain affected area
Cannot diagnose or prescribe
↑ Always refer to IBCLC + BFLM

  • Assess for clinical mastitis presentation
  • Optimize milk removal plan
  • Support continuing to breastfeed
  • Recommend physician contact urgently
↑ Must refer to BFLM / PCP for antibiotics
Primary role
  • Diagnose lactational mastitis
  • Prescribe appropriate antibiotics
  • Rule out abscess (ultrasound if needed)
  • Manage inflammatory mastitis (non-infectious)
  • Follow up to resolution
Postpartum mood & D-MER
Low mood with letdown; anxiety; feeling dread while nursing
Important role
  • Emotional check-ins & listening
  • Normalize mood fluctuations
  • Encourage help-seeking
Can help
  • Recognize D-MER pattern
  • Provide educational resources
  • Validate experience, reduce shame
↑ Refer to IBCLC or BFLM for D-MER
Can help
  • Clinical recognition of D-MER
  • Screen for PPD / PPA overlap
  • Education on dopamine mechanism

Primary role
  • Diagnose D-MER or PPA/PPD/PMADs

  • Coordinate with OB, MFM, psychiatry
  • Medication safety in lactation counseling

Doula
Emotional, practical, & informational support. No clinical assessment or diagnosis. Vital for confidence-building & early problem identification.
CLC — Certified Lactation Counselor
Trained in common lactation issues. Can observe, educate & provide hands-on guidance. Cannot diagnose medical conditions.
IBCLC — Int'l Board Certified Lactation Consultant
Highest lactation-specific credential. Clinical assessment, latch evaluation, milk transfer measurement. Still cannot diagnose or prescribe.
BFLM Clinician — Breastfeeding / Lactation Medicine
Licensed clinician with additional lactation medicine training (ABM). Can diagnose conditions, prescribe medications, and manage complex or medical breastfeeding problems. NABBLM-C are board certified specialists. All BFLM physicians can coordinate with OB/GYN and primary care physicians or other specialists/medical team members as needed.
